Norbert Wiener has no less than 19 theorems named after him in mathematics.


He wrote a fiction book called The Tempter published in 1959, which was a book trying to communicate to his readers a description of the complex interaction among science, engineering and business.

He had some famous students, some of which are:

  • Amar Bose, founder and former chairman of Bose Corporation.
  • Colin Cherry, a British cognitive scientist focused on auditory attention, especially cocktail party problem.
  • Shikao Ikehara, a prominent Japanese mathematician who translated one of his books to Japanese
  • Norman Levinson, an American mathematician with significant contribution in signal processing.
